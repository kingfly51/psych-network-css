# Local cross-sectional exemplar

The workspace reference is:

Zhang, P., Wang, L., Zhou, Q., et al. (2023). A network analysis of anxiety and depression symptoms in Chinese disabled elderly. *Journal of Affective Disorders, 333*, 535-542. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jad.2023.04.065

Use it as a structural exemplar, not as a source of results for a new dataset. Its relevant organization is:

- Methods: network estimation; expected influence for signed networks; node predictability; bridge expected influence; nonparametric edge-weight bootstrap; case-dropping stability; difference tests.
- Results: sample/descriptives; network density and strongest edges; predictability; CS coefficients and bootstrap precision; central and bridge symptoms; optional group comparison.

Methodological sources named in the paper that may be cited after verifying against the source or DOI include:

- Epskamp, S., Borsboom, D., & Fried, E. I. (2018). Estimating psychological networks and their accuracy: A tutorial paper. *Behavior Research Methods, 50*, 195-212. https://doi.org/10.3758/s13428-017-0862-1
- Epskamp, S., Cramer, A. O. J., Waldorp, L. J., Schmittmann, V. D., & Borsboom, D. (2012). qgraph: Network visualizations of relationships in psychometric data. *Journal of Statistical Software, 48*(4), 1-18. https://doi.org/10.18637/jss.v048.i04
- Robinaugh, D. J., Millner, A. J., & McNally, R. J. (2016). Identifying highly influential nodes in the complicated grief network. *Journal of Abnormal Psychology, 125*(6), 747-757. https://doi.org/10.1037/abn0000181
- Jones, P. J., Ma, R., & McNally, R. J. (2021). Bridge centrality: A network approach to understanding comorbidity. *Multivariate Behavioral Research, 56*(2), 353-367. https://doi.org/10.1080/00273171.2019.1614898
- Haslbeck, J. M. B., & Waldorp, L. J. (2018). How well do network models predict observations? On the importance of predictability in network models. *Behavior Research Methods, 50*, 853-861. https://doi.org/10.3758/s13428-017-0910-x

Do not reproduce the exemplar's methodological imprecision that a GGM itself handles ordered/categorical data. For ordinal data, explicitly report the polychoric association step; for binary data, prefer an Ising model; for mixed data, use an MGM.
